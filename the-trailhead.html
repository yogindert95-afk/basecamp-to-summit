The problem

The first two years decide the next ten.

Campus rewards knowing the answer. Work rewards something else entirely — communicating clearly, reading the room, handling feedback, and delivering without someone checking your homework. Most graduates learn this by tripping over it.

The Trailhead compresses those first hard years into weeks of deliberate practice, so you arrive looking like someone worth promoting.

Who this is for

Three people standing at the trailhead.

The final-year student

Placements are coming and your CV says "team player" without evidence. You want to walk into interviews — and your first job — with skills the other candidates don't have yet.

The fresh graduate

You've landed the job and the first months feel like a foreign country: unwritten rules, vague feedback, meetings you don't know how to use. You want to find your feet fast — and get noticed for the right reasons.

The college or placement cell

Your students are academically strong but employers keep saying "not workplace-ready." We run The Trailhead as a cohort on campus — practical, measurable, and shaped to the industries your students enter.

The route in

From classroom to colleague, one stage at a time.

The Trailhead adapts the same enterprise training used for new professionals at global firms — cut down to what actually matters in your first two years.

4 stages Cohort or campus program Practice, not lectures Interview-to-first-90-days

Open each stage to see how you learn it — and how you practise it.

1

Speak like a professional

+

The single biggest gap employers report. Say things clearly, to the right person, at the right altitude — in writing, in meetings, and under pressure.
What you'll learn
Communicating up, down & across
How the same message changes for a peer, a manager, and a client — and why "reply all" is a decision.
Social Styles
Read the personalities in the room — Analytical, Amiable, Driving, Expressive — and flex to each.
Speaking up early
Contributing in meetings when you're the most junior person there.
You'll practise
  • Rewrite a real email three ways for three audiences.
  • Present for two minutes and take live questions.
2

Own your work

+

Nobody assigns homework anymore. Manage your time, your tasks, and your commitments so things you touch get finished.
What you'll learn
The 4 D's
Every task gets Dumped, Delegated, Done, or Date-activated — nothing just lingers.
Managing your manager
Setting expectations, asking for help before deadlines (not after), and making your work visible.
Calendar as home base
Plan by priority, not by inbox — and protect the time that matters.
You'll practise
  • Sort a real week's workload through the 4 D's.
  • Run a check-in conversation with a "manager" role-play.
3

Work with others

+

Teams, feedback, and friction — the parts of work no exam ever tested, and the ones that decide your reputation.
What you'll learn
Taking feedback well
Hearing criticism as information, responding without defensiveness, and acting on it visibly.
Active Listening
The five levels — from "waiting to talk" to actually hearing someone.
Working across difference
Collaborating with people who don't think, work, or communicate like you.
You'll practise
  • Receive live feedback on a piece of your work — and act on it.
  • Run a listening exercise in pairs, both seats.
4

Land the role — and the first 90 days

+

Interviews are a skill, and so is arriving. Walk in prepared, and spend your first three months building the reputation you'll trade on for years.
What you'll learn
Behaviour-based interviews, from the other side
What interviewers are actually scoring — and how to give them evidence, not adjectives.
The first-90-days plan
Learn the landscape, find the real influencers, deliver one visible win.
Your professional brand
What you want to be known for by month three — chosen on purpose.
You'll practise
  • Full mock interview with structured scoring and feedback.
  • Write your own first-90-days plan.
