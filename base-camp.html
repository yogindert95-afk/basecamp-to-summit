Who this is for

Base Camp is built for three climbers.

The ready IC

You're the strongest person on the team and the promotion conversations have started. You want to walk into your first leadership role already knowing how to run a 1:1, give feedback, and coach — not learn it on your team's time.

The first-year manager

Promoted in the last two years, leading 3–10 people, and privately overwhelmed. You need the practical mechanics — delegation, difficult conversations, priorities under pressure — not another leadership book.

The organisation building its bench

You're an HR or L&D leader who keeps promoting your best ICs and watching the first year hurt. We run Base Camp as a cohort for your aspiring and new managers — tied to real promotion decisions.

The route

Every stage of the climb — and what you learn at each.

Base Camp merges the Aspiring Manager and New Manager Academies — programs refined across thousands of enterprise L&D hours. The climb runs from the identity shift of becoming a manager to the storms of actually being one.

7 stages 3–6 weeks 2–3 hrs each Half teaching, half workshop

This is one example of how a longer-term program can run — modules, pacing, and depth are shaped to your cohort and goals.

Open each stage to see how you learn it — and how you practise it.

1
Make the shift

Make the transition

+

The hardest step on the mountain — from strongest climber to leading the rope team. Rewire where your value now comes from.
What you'll learn
Manager vs Leader
The real difference between keeping things running and taking people somewhere new.
Transition to Management
The mindset, time, and identity shifts nobody warns a new manager about.
Psychological Safety
Why teams only perform when it's safe to speak up, and how you set that tone from day one.
You'll practise
  • Write your own leadership brand statement.
  • Map where your time goes now vs where it should — and run a first 1:1 that builds safety, not status.
2

Communicate powerfully

+

Read the people in front of you and adjust how you land a message, so intent and impact finally line up.
What you'll learn
Social Styles
Read people on two axes — assertiveness and responsiveness — as Analytical, Amiable, Driving or Expressive, then flex to each.
Mission of Discovery
A conversation that surfaces what someone actually needs before you respond.
Action · Impact · Outcome
A clean structure for feedback that lands without defensiveness.
You'll practise
  • Map your own style and your team's.
  • Run a Mission of Discovery conversation.
3
Manage your team

Build a great team

+

Hire on evidence, not gut — then build the safety and belonging that make a team want to climb with you.
What you'll learn
Behaviour-Based Interviewing
Past behaviour predicts future behaviour — score candidates against a traits model instead of a gut feel.
Leading with Empathy
The foundation of a team that feels safe enough to take real risks.
The Happiness Index
A simple, repeatable read on how your team is actually doing.
You'll practise
  • Run a behaviour-based interview.
  • Take your team's Happiness Index.
4

Coach & delegate

+

Stop solving every problem yourself. Ask the right questions, hand off real work, and hold the rope so people find their own footing.
What you'll learn
Active Listening
The five levels — from "not listening" to truly hearing someone in their world.
Will vs Skill
A matrix that tells you when to direct, coach, delegate or motivate.
GROW coaching & Delegation
Ask instead of tell — and match the task to the person, letting go without dropping the rope.
You'll practise
  • Place each team member on the Will/Skill matrix.
  • Delegate a real task using a clear handoff.
5
Manage yourself

Improve your results

+

Match your style to what each person needs, and run work and performance so the whole team moves as one.
What you'll learn
Situational Leadership
There's no single right style — match yours to the person and the task in front of you.
Leading projects
Running work so momentum is shared, not carried by you alone.
Performance Management
Turning goals into fair, clear, motivating reviews.
You'll practise
  • Plan roles & priorities for a team member.
  • Draft a performance management plan.
6

Climb through storms

+

Stay calm and clear when conditions turn — leading change, holding priorities, and working through conflict.
What you'll learn
Leading Change · the Switch model
Why change stalls, and how to move a team when the head and the heart pull in different directions.
Prioritization
Protect what matters when everything feels urgent.
Managing Conflict
Name tension early and turn it into a better outcome.
You'll practise
  • Build a Switch plan for a real change facing your team.
  • Role-play a difficult team conversation.
7

Decide what to do

+

Cut the noise. Choose what matters first, protect the time for it, and work a problem down to a decision.
What you'll learn
Productivity Principles
Consolidate, plan, decide — get to one single source of "work" instead of ten.
The 4 D's
Every task gets Dumped, Delegated, Done, or Date-activated — nothing just lingers.
Time Management
Build a "home base": start with your calendar, prioritise by task not inbox, cut the distractions.
You'll practise
  • Sort a real workload through the 4 D's.
  • Design a calendar that protects what matters — and decide what NOT to do.
The summit push · final exercise
Lead a real challenge, start to finish.

Every climber closes with a genuine management scenario — worked in writing and in a live role-play. It's where the fundamentals become instinct, and the results can feed straight into promotion decisions.

Questions at Base Camp

Before you rope up.

Is this online or in person?+

Both. Base Camp runs as in-person workshops on-site or as live online cohorts. We'll pick the format that fits your people on the call.

How big is a group?+

Small enough that no one hides at the back — typically 12 to 30. Larger cohorts are split so everyone gets time on the rope.

I haven't started managing yet — is it too early?+

No — that's the best time. The early stages are built for people about to lead; the later stages give you a preview of the storms so they don't surprise you. If you're still a student or brand new to corporate life, start at The Trailhead instead.

Do we prepare anything first?+

A short reflection before day one, so people arrive knowing what they want to work on. No reading list, no busywork — the climbing happens in the room.
